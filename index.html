Research Interests

My research spans computational modeling, sustainable materials design, and advanced manufacturing. I investigate how atomic-scale simulation and data-driven modeling can accelerate the discovery and optimization of next-generation materials and devices.

Density Functional Theory (DFT) and Electronic Structure

Simulation of perovskite and hybrid materials for optoelectronic and energy applications.

Biodegradable and Sustainable Composites

Use of natural fillers and polymer matrices to achieve mechanical performance with reduced environmental impact.

Computational Materials Design

Data-driven property prediction and multi-objective optimization using machine learning.

Three-Dimensional CAD and Product Design

Modeling, visualization, and design-for-manufacture using SolidWorks, AutoCAD, and KeyShot.

Advanced Characterization and Simulation Coupling

Linking first-principles results with experimental measurements to validate structure–property relationships.

Nanomaterials and Smart Polymers

Investigation of nanostructured and stimuli-responsive systems for biomedical and flexible devices.

Selected Projects

DFT Simulation of ASiF3 Perovskites

First‑author project studying pressure‑tuned band gap and optical behavior of ASiF3 (A = Na, K, Rb) perovskites using Density Functional Theory for optoelectronic applications.

Eggshell Filler Hybrid Composites

Mechanical, fracture, and biodegradability study of hybrid fiber–reinforced composites using eggshell as a sustainable filler, combining ASTM‑based testing with SEM fracture analysis (ICCHE 2023).

Material Selection for Heat Exchangers

Poster‑level study using CES EduPack to select heat‑exchanger materials with emphasis on thermal efficiency, corrosion resistance, and cost optimization for industrial applications.

NiMH Battery Materials Study

Course project reviewing working principles, materials choices, and performance considerations of nickel–metal hydride batteries for energy storage.
